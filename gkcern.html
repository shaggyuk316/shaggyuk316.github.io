GKCERN Simulation

GKCERN simulates particle collisions like those at CERN’s Large Hadron Collider, with 8 particle types: Protons, Quarks, Muons, Photons, Electrons, Neutrinos, Gluons, and Higgs Bosons. Adjust settings in the Mixer to control particle dynamics and observe color mixing when particles collide.

Protons Colliding

Protons move in a circular path within the collider ring, colliding at the center to simulate high-energy particle interactions. Adjust settings in the Mixer to control speed and count.

Learning Outcome: Learn how particle accelerators use high-speed collisions.

Quarks Scattering

Quarks scatter outward in jets after collisions, with color mixing when they collide with other particles. Use the Mixer to adjust density and color.

Learning Outcome: Understand the role of quarks and color mixing in particle physics.

Muons Curving

Muons follow curved paths due to the magnetic field, eventually decaying. Adjust magnetic field strength in the Mixer.

Learning Outcome: Discover how magnetic fields identify particles like muons.

Photons Emitting

Collisions emit photons as glowing energy bursts. Control emission intensity in the Mixer.

Learning Outcome: Explore how energy is released as photons.

Electrons Moving

Electrons move at high speed, affected by magnetic fields. Adjust settings in the Mixer.

Learning Outcome: Learn about electron behavior in colliders.

Neutrinos Passing

Neutrinos pass through matter, barely interacting. Adjust visibility in the Mixer.

Learning Outcome: Understand neutrinos’ weak interactions.

Gluons Binding

Gluons connect quarks, visualized as lines. Adjust density in the Mixer.

Learning Outcome: Explore the role of gluons in particle physics.

Higgs Bosons Decaying

Higgs bosons appear briefly and decay. Control decay rate in the Mixer.

Learning Outcome: Learn about the Higgs Boson’s role in imparting mass.
